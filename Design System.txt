# The Design System: Editorial High-Performance Guidelines

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
The Creative North Star for this design system is **"The Neon Laboratory."**

This isn't a standard corporate dashboard; it is a high-performance environment that mimics the precision of automated intelligence. We move beyond the "template" look by utilizing deep atmospheric depth, high-contrast neon accents, and a layout that favors intentional negative space and asymmetric content blocks. The goal is to make the user feel like they are operating a futuristic command center where every pixel vibrates with energy and purpose.

Through the use of **Tonal Layering** and **Luminous Accents**, we replace rigid structures with fluid, light-based hierarchy.

---

## 2. Colors & Atmospheric Depth
Our palette is rooted in the deep void of space, punctuated by the high-frequency energy of artificial light.

### The Foundation
- **Background:** `#14121b` (The Void)
- **Surface Tiers:** Use `surface-container-low` (`#1c1a24`) through `highest` (`#35333e`) to define spatial hierarchy.

### The Accents (Light Emission)
- **Primary:** `primary` (`#d0bcff`) & `primary_container` (`#a078ff`) — Used for intelligence and automation indicators.
- **Secondary:** `secondary` (`#45dfa4`) — Used for growth, success, and high-performance metrics.
- **Tertiary:** `tertiary` (`#fbabff`) — Used for disruptive calls to action and "human" intervention points.

### Strategic Guidelines
* **The "No-Line" Rule:** 1px solid borders are prohibited for sectioning. Contrast must be achieved through background shifts. For example, a `surface-container-low` card should sit directly on a `surface` background. The eye should perceive the boundary through the shift in darkness, not a line.
* **Surface Hierarchy & Nesting:** Treat the UI as stacked sheets of obsidian glass. An inner component (like a code snippet) should use a `surface-container-lowest` to feel "recessed" into the UI, while a floating modal uses `surface-bright` to feel "closer" to the user.
* **The "Glass & Gradient" Rule:** Primary buttons and hero sections must utilize linear gradients (e.g., `primary` to `tertiary`) with a `backdrop-blur` of 12px to 20px when floating. This creates "visual soul"—a depth that flat colors lack.

---

## 3. Typography: The Editorial Voice
We utilize a high-contrast typographic scale to separate data from narrative.

* **Display & Headlines (Plus Jakarta Sans):** These are our "Impact" layers. Use `display-lg` for hero statements with tight letter-spacing (-0.02em). This typeface carries the "Futuristic" weight of the system.
* **Body & Titles (Inter):** Our "Utility" layer. Inter provides maximum readability at small sizes. Ensure `body-md` uses a comfortable line-height (1.6) to provide breathing room within dense data.
* **Labels (Space Grotesk):** Our "Technical" layer. Used for mono-spaced metadata, system status, and small badges. This reinforces the "automated" vibe.

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth: Tonal Layering
Traditional dropshadows are too heavy for this system. We use **Ambient Luminous Shadows**.

* **The Layering Principle:** Depth is achieved by stacking.
1. Base: `surface`
2. Section: `surface-container-low`
3. Card: `surface-container-high`
* **Ambient Shadows:** For floating elements, use a diffused shadow with an 8% opacity. The shadow color must be a tint of `primary` or `tertiary` rather than black, creating a "glow" rather than a shadow.
* **The "Ghost Border" Fallback:** If containment is required for accessibility, use `outline-variant` at 15% opacity. It should be felt, not seen.
* **Glassmorphism:** Navigation bars and floating action buttons must use a semi-transparent `surface` color with a heavy blur to allow the neon background accents to bleed through, integrating the UI into the environment.

## 5. Components

### Buttons (The Energy Core)
* **Primary:** A vibrant gradient from `#8B5CF6` to `#D946EF`. Add a subtle outer glow using the same colors.
* **Secondary:** Ghost style. No background, `outline` border at 20% opacity, with `secondary` text color.
* **Tertiary:** `on_surface_variant` text, no border, used for low-priority utility actions.

### Cards (The Data Pods)
* **Construction:** Use `surface-container-high`. No borders. Use `xl` (1.5rem) roundedness for a modern, friendly-tech feel.
* **Hover State:** Shift the background to `surface-bright` and increase the glow-shadow intensity.

### Input Fields (The Command Entry)
* **Style:** `surface-container-lowest` background to create a recessed feel.
* **Active State:** The bottom border animates to a `primary` gradient. Label moves to `label-sm` using `Space Grotesk`.

### Chips & Badges
* **Execution:** Small, uppercase `Space Grotesk` text. Backgrounds should be 10% opacity versions of the status color (e.g., 10% `secondary` for a "Live" status), ensuring the text remains the focal point.

---

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### Do:
* **Use Asymmetry:** Place a large headline on the left with a significantly smaller technical description on the right to create editorial tension.
* **Embrace the Dark:** Allow large areas of `#07060E` to exist. Negative space is a luxury in high-end design.
* **Use Color for Intent:** Only use `#34D399` (Secondary) for successful automation or growth metrics.

### Don't:
* **Don't use 100% White:** For body text, use `on_surface_variant` (`#cbc3d7`). Pure white (`#FFFFFF`) should be reserved only for the highest-level headings.
* **Don't use Dividers:** Avoid horizontal lines between list items. Use the spacing scale (`8` or `12`) to create separation through white space.
* **Don't over-saturate:** If every element has a neon glow, nothing is important. Use neon strictly for interaction points and key data highlights.
